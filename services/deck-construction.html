Decking materials we build with

We’ll recommend the best fit based on sun exposure, maintenance tolerance, and budget — then build it right.

Composite decking (Trex® / TimberTech®)

Low-maintenance, long-lasting, and consistent color. Ideal if you want a clean look without annual upkeep.

  • Low maintenance: no sanding or staining required.
  • Resists rot + insects and holds up well over time.
  • Modern finishes that pair well with black railings and clean lines.

Cedar & pressure-treated pine

Real wood warmth and natural character. Cedar is a favorite for its look and performance in Texas.

  • Cedar: naturally resistant and beautiful with stain or natural aging.
  • Treated pine: budget-friendly, strong, and easy to maintain with stain.

Ipe hardwood (premium)

Ultra-durable, dense, and stunning. If you want a “forever deck” feel, Ipe is hard to beat.

  • Extremely durable and naturally resistant to rot/insects.
  • Rich color that looks incredible with black railing systems.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best decking material for the Texas climate?

Composite is great for low maintenance. Cedar is a classic Texas performer. Ipe is premium durability with a luxury look.

Do I need a permit to build a deck in Austin, TX?

Often, yes—especially if attached or elevated. Requirements vary by city and scope. We’ll guide you through the process.

How much does a new deck cost in Austin?

It depends on size, height, framing complexity, and materials. We provide a detailed free estimate so there are no surprises.

Can you build stairs, railings, and pergolas too?

Yes — stairs, landings, modern black railings, privacy screens, and pergolas are common add-ons we build.
