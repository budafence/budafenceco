Common Fence Problems We Repair

Broken or Rotted Posts

Our #1 repair request. Fence posts often rot at ground level or break during storms. We replace damaged posts with new posts set properly in concrete for a lasting repair.

Leaning Fence Sections

Often caused by rotted wood posts or posts that weren't set properly in concrete. We can reset or replace posts to straighten your fence.

Storm Damage

High winds and falling debris are a leading cause of fence damage in Central Texas. We respond quickly to storm damage calls.

Sagging or Misaligned Gates

Caused by hinge failure, post settling, or a gate that's too heavy for its frame. We can reinforce, rehang, or rebuild your gate.

Damaged Pickets or Panels

Individual pickets or entire sections damaged by storms, falling limbs, or accidents. We match existing materials for seamless repairs.

Rot and Insect Damage

Wood fences can suffer from rot at the base or damage from termites. We remove damaged sections and replace with treated materials.

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can you repair my fence?

Most small to medium fence repairs can be completed within 48 hours of your call. We offer same-day and emergency repair services with proper notice. Larger repairs may need to be scheduled based on the scope of work.

What types of fence damage do you repair?

We repair all types of fence damage including storm damage, broken or rotted posts, leaning fence sections, damaged pickets and panels, sagging gates, and rot or insect damage. We work on wood, metal, chain link, and welded fences.

Can you repair a fence that another company installed?

Yes, absolutely. We can repair any type of wood, metal, or chain link fence, regardless of who originally installed it. We'll match the existing materials as closely as possible.

What is your most common fence repair?

Post replacement is our number one repair request. Fence posts often rot at ground level or break during storms. We replace damaged wood posts with new posts set properly in concrete for a lasting repair.

My gate is sagging. Can it be fixed?

In most cases, yes. Sagging gates are usually caused by loose hinges, a settling post, or a frame that has come out of square. We can typically fix this by reinforcing the gate, replacing the hardware, or resetting the post.

How much does fence repair cost?

Fence repair costs vary depending on the extent of damage. Simple picket replacements are very affordable, while multiple post replacements will cost more. We provide free, no-obligation quotes for all repair work.

Do you offer emergency fence repair?

Yes, we offer same-day and emergency fence repair services. We understand that a damaged fence can be a security risk for your family and pets. Call us at (512) 710-7116 for urgent repairs.
