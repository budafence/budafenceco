Chain Link Fence Options

Galvanized Chain Link

Classic silver/gray chain link with a zinc coating to protect against rust. Great for large properties, commercial sites, and budget-focused installs.

Pros

  • Lowest cost per linear foot
  • Strong rust protection
  • Great for large areas
  • 20–30 year lifespan

Cons

  • More “industrial” appearance
  • Not as hidden in landscaping

Black Vinyl-Coated Chain Link

A premium option with a PVC coating over galvanized steel. It looks cleaner, blends into landscaping, and adds another layer of protection.

Pros

  • Best aesthetics (popular for homes)
  • Extra rust protection
  • Smoother finish
  • Often 30+ year lifespan

Cons

  • Higher initial cost
  • May fade slightly over many years

Privacy Slats & Windscreen

Want privacy without a full wood fence? Slats and windscreen fabric can reduce visibility while keeping chain link’s strength and airflow. Perfect for trash enclosures, pool areas, and backyards.

Our Professional Installation Process

A chain link fence is only as good as the install. We build a rigid framework, stretch the mesh tight, and finish clean.

1

Layout & Planning

We confirm boundaries, grade, gate locations, and help you choose the best height, gauge, and finish.

2

Heavy-Duty Posts + Extra Concrete

Terminal posts (corners/ends/gates) get the strength. Proper depth and concrete volume prevents lean and future sag.

3

Top Rail & Hardware

We use a stronger top rail and solid hardware so your fence stays tight and stable for years.

4

Stretch the Mesh Tight

We stretch chain link properly with the right tools—no loose fabric, no rattling, no sloppy finish.

5

Final Walkthrough & Cleanup

We verify alignment, gate swing/latch, and leave your property clean. Then we back it with our warranty.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a chain link fence last?

Galvanized chain link typically lasts 20–30 years. Vinyl-coated options often last even longer.

What heights are available?

We install 4–10 ft chain link fencing. Most residential jobs are 4–6 ft; commercial/security is often 8–10 ft.

Can I add privacy?

Yes. Privacy slats and windscreen fabric are common upgrades for backyards and enclosures.

Is chain link good for dogs?

Absolutely. It’s strong, secure, and great for pet containment. We can also recommend options to reduce digging.

How fast can you install it?

Many residential projects install around 150–200 ft/day depending on terrain, access, and gate layout.
