Why Choose a Chain Link Fence?

Chain link fences are chosen for their practical benefits. They offer an unbeatable combination of affordability, durability, and low maintenance that makes them a smart choice for a wide range of applications.

Unbeatable Value

Chain link is the most cost-effective fencing material available. It provides a high level of security and durability for a significantly lower initial investment compared to other fence types, making it perfect for large areas or budget-conscious projects. A standard 4-foot galvanized chain link fence can be 30-50% less expensive than a 6-foot wood privacy fence.

Durability and Longevity

Built to last, our chain link fences are made from heavy-gauge steel (9-gauge or 11-gauge options) that has been galvanized (coated in zinc) to prevent rust and corrosion. This tough material can withstand the harsh Texas weather and requires virtually no maintenance to keep performing for 20-30 years or more.

High Security

The interwoven steel mesh creates a formidable barrier that is difficult to cut or climb. It's an excellent choice for securing commercial properties, industrial sites, and backyards, providing a clear line of sight while keeping intruders out. For enhanced security, we can add barbed wire to the top.

Zero Maintenance

A chain link fence is the definition of "set it and forget it." It never needs painting, staining, or sealing. It won't rot, warp, or be damaged by insects. An occasional rinse is all that's needed to keep it looking its best.

Fast Installation

Chain link fences are faster to install than wood or metal fences. We can typically complete 150-200 linear feet in one day for standard residential projects, getting your property secured quickly.

Chain Link Fence Options

Modern chain link comes in several varieties, allowing you to choose the right balance of performance and aesthetics for your needs.

Galvanized Chain Link

This is the traditional and most common type of chain link fence. The steel components are hot-dipped in zinc, a process called galvanization, which provides a thick, durable coating that protects the steel from rust and corrosion for many years.

Pros:

  • Most affordable option - lowest cost per linear foot
  • Highly durable with excellent rust protection
  • Classic, utilitarian look - standard for commercial use
  • 20-30 year lifespan

Cons:

  • Industrial appearance with silver/gray finish
  • May not suit all residential aesthetics

Vinyl-Coated Chain Link

For a more refined look, consider a vinyl-coated chain link fence. In this option, the galvanized steel core is coated in a layer of colored vinyl (PVC). This provides an extra layer of protection against rust and a more attractive finish.

Pros:

  • Enhanced aesthetics - available in black, green, brown, and custom colors
  • Superior durability with double rust protection
  • Smoother finish - more comfortable to touch
  • Blends naturally into landscaping
  • 30+ year lifespan

Cons:

  • Higher cost than standard galvanized
  • Vinyl coating can fade slightly over time in direct sun

Privacy Slats & Windscreen Fabric

Want the durability of chain link with the privacy of a solid fence? Add privacy slats or windscreen fabric to your chain link fence. These vinyl strips are inserted into the mesh to obstruct the view, providing privacy while maintaining airflow. They're especially popular for trash can enclosures, pool areas, and residential backyards.

Available in multiple colors to match your fence or blend with your surroundings. Windscreen fabric is also excellent for reducing wind exposure in open areas.

Our Professional Installation Process

A chain link fence is only as strong as its installation. Our process ensures your fence is taut, secure, and built to last.

1

Consultation and Layout

We start by understanding your needs and accurately measuring your property. We help you choose the right height (4ft to 10ft), gauge (9 or 11), and coating (galvanized or vinyl) for your application.

2

Setting the Foundation

The strength of a chain link fence is in its terminal posts (corner, end, and gate posts). We use heavy-duty corner posts and line posts, setting them deep in extra concrete to create a rigid framework that will not sag. This is a critical step that many competitors skip.

3

Installing Top Rail and Hardware

We install a 1-5/8" top rail (not the cheaper 1-3/8" that some companies use) for superior strength and stability. We then attach all necessary hardware, including tension bands and wires to hold the mesh tight.

4

Stretching the Mesh

This is where expertise matters. We use specialized tools to stretch the chain link mesh until it is perfectly taut between the terminal posts. A properly stretched fence will not sag or rattle. We then secure the mesh to the posts and rails with steel ties.

5

Final Inspection and Cleanup

We conduct a thorough inspection to ensure the fence is perfectly aligned, taut, and secure. We clean up all debris and materials, leaving your property clean and your new fence ready to use.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a chain link fence last?

A professionally installed galvanized chain link fence can easily last 20-30 years. A vinyl-coated fence can last even longer due to its extra layer of protection against rust and corrosion.

What heights are available for chain link fences?

We install chain link fences from 4 feet tall up to 10 feet tall. Standard residential heights are 4, 5, and 6 feet. For commercial or security applications, we can install fences 8-10 feet tall, often with the addition of barbed wire for added security.

Can you add privacy slats to a chain link fence?

Yes, privacy slats and windscreen fabric are popular additions to chain link fences. These vinyl strips are inserted into the mesh to obstruct the view, providing privacy. They're especially nice in areas like trash can enclosures and are available in various colors to match your fence or surroundings.

Is chain link a good choice for dogs?

Chain link is an excellent choice for dog enclosures. It's strong, durable, and provides a secure area for your pets to run and play. We can ensure the fence is installed flush with the ground to prevent digging underneath.

How does the cost compare to a wood fence?

A standard 4-foot galvanized chain link fence can be 30-50% less expensive than a standard 6-foot wood privacy fence, making it an extremely economical choice, especially for large properties or commercial applications.

Do you install commercial chain link fences?

Yes, we install chain link fences for residential, commercial, agricultural, and security applications. We offer heavy-duty options with higher gauge steel and can add barbed wire for enhanced security on commercial and industrial properties.

How fast can you install a chain link fence?

Chain link fences are faster to install than wood or metal fences. We can typically install 150-200 linear feet in one day for standard residential projects, getting your property secured quickly without sacrificing quality.
