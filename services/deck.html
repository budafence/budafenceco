The Right Materials for Your Texas Deck

Choosing the right material is the most important step in designing a deck that lasts. We specialize in materials proven to withstand the harsh Central Texas climate of intense sun and occasional heavy rain.

Composite Decking (Trex®, TimberTech®)

For a low-maintenance deck that resists fading, staining, and rot, composite is an unbeatable choice. We are experienced installers of all major brands, including Trex, TimberTech, and Deckorators.

  • Extremely Low Maintenance: No sanding or staining required.
  • Durable & Long-Lasting: Resists rot, insects, and weather damage.
  • Wide Variety of Colors & Styles: Mimics the look of real wood without the upkeep.

Classic Wood Decks

The timeless beauty of a real wood deck is undeniable. We use only high-quality lumber to ensure a beautiful and long-lasting result.

  • Cedar: Naturally beautiful and resistant to rot and insects. Ages to a graceful silver-gray or can be stained.
  • Pressure-Treated Pine: A cost-effective and durable option that can be stained to match your home.
  • Ipe (Brazilian Hardwood): The ultimate in luxury and durability. Ipe is one of the hardest woods available, with a 50+ year lifespan and a stunning, rich color.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best decking material for the Texas climate?

Both composite decking (like Trex) and hardwoods (like Ipe) are excellent choices for Texas. Composites offer low maintenance and heat resistance, while Ipe is incredibly durable and naturally resistant to rot and insects. Cedar is also a great, classic option that performs well in the heat.

Do I need a permit to build a deck in Austin, TX?

In Austin, a permit is typically required for decks that are more than 30 inches above the ground, attached to the house, or covered by a roof. We handle the entire permitting process for you, ensuring your project is fully compliant with local building codes.

How much does a new deck cost in Austin?

Deck costs vary widely based on size, materials, and complexity. Treated pine is the most budget-friendly, cedar is a mid-range option, and composite and Ipe are premium choices. We provide a detailed, free estimate with no hidden fees.
