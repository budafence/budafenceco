Wood Fence Materials: Cedar vs. Pressure-Treated Pine

The most critical decision you'll make is the type of wood for your fence. We primarily work with Western Red Cedar and pressure-treated Southern Yellow Pine, as they are the best options for durability and value in our climate.

Western Red Cedar

Often considered the premium choice for wood fencing, Western Red Cedar contains natural oils that make it inherently resistant to rot, decay, and insects.

Pros:

  • • Natural durability without chemicals
  • • Dimensional stability - stays straight
  • • Stunning appearance with natural aging
  • • Lightweight and easy to work with

Cons:

  • • Higher initial cost than pine

Pressure-Treated Pine

A more budget-friendly option that offers excellent durability. Chemical preservatives are forced deep into the wood's fibers for long-lasting protection.

Pros:

  • • Cost-effective and budget-friendly
  • • Excellent durability with treatment
  • • Readily available resource
  • • Great value for larger projects

Cons:

  • • Can warp or crack as it dries
  • • Requires staining for best appearance

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a cedar fence last in Texas?

A well-built cedar fence can last 20-25 years in the Central Texas climate. The key factors are the quality of the materials and the installation. Using steel posts, which we do on all our fences, can significantly extend the life of the fence by preventing rot at the base.

How long does wood fence installation take?

Most residential wood fence projects under 100 linear feet are completed in one day. Larger projects may take 2-3 days depending on terrain, complexity, and weather conditions. We'll provide an accurate timeline during your free consultation.

Do I need to stain my wood fence?

While not required, staining your wood fence protects it from UV rays and moisture, significantly extending its lifespan and maintaining its appearance. We recommend staining within the first year and reapplying every 2-3 years. We offer professional fence staining as an optional upgrade service.

What is the difference between cedar and treated pine?

Cedar is naturally rot-resistant with a premium appearance and better dimensional stability, while treated pine is more budget-friendly with chemical preservatives for durability. Both are excellent choices for the Texas climate. Cedar ages to a beautiful silver-gray, while pine requires staining to prevent graying.

Do you remove and haul away old fences?

Yes! We demo and haul away your old existing fence at no extra charge. This is included in all our installation quotes. We'll remove the old fence, dispose of it properly, and leave your property clean.

Do you handle permits?

Absolutely. Any permits required by your municipality are handled by Buda Fence Company. We take care of all the paperwork and coordination with local authorities so you don't have to worry about it.

What is your installation guarantee?

Every wood fence installation is backed by our 5-year installation guarantee. This covers workmanship and structural integrity. We stand behind our work because we build fences to last.
